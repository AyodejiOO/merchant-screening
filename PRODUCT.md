# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Compliance and risk analysts at a fintech / payments platform. They use this tool daily as part of merchant onboarding and ongoing monitoring workflows: running batch screenings, reviewing fuzzy match results, clearing merchants, or escalating flagged names. They are expert operators who need precision and speed, not handholding.

## Product Purpose

Internal sanction screening tool that checks merchants against 6 global sanctions lists (OFAC, EU, UN, UK, Australia, Canada). Surfaces matches with confidence scores, supports single lookups and bulk CSV batch jobs, syncs lists automatically, and stores screening history for audit purposes.

Success looks like: a compliance analyst can run a batch of 500 merchants, filter to confirmed matches, review each one, and export a report in under 10 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Fast, precise, trustworthy. Internal operations tooling: it should feel native to the company that runs it, not like an off-the-shelf SaaS product.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS (Intercom, Notion, Loom style): pastel cards, rounded-everything, purple gradients, smiley illustrations. This tool makes serious compliance decisions.
- Government / banking form UIs: grey, dense, zero hierarchy, hostile to scanning.
- Security dashboard drama: neon red alerts, dark-mode theatrics. Routine screening shouldn't feel like a DEFCON monitor.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed over ceremony.** Every click costs an analyst's time. Reduce steps, surface the most important action without requiring navigation.
2. **Signal, not noise.** Match status (confirmed / potential / clear) must be readable in under 1 second. Everything else is secondary.
3. **Earn trust through precision.** Consistent spacing, readable data tables, no ambiguity in labels. If a number is wrong by even one, it undermines the whole tool.
4. **Native, not generic.** Use a consistent, restrained brand as the visual foundation. This is an internal product, but it should feel cohesive and purpose-built, not like an off-the-shelf template.
5. **Experts only.** No onboarding tooltips, no empty-state hand-holding on repeat visits. Trust the analyst.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic compliance for an internal tool. Sufficient contrast for data-heavy tables, keyboard navigation for screening workflows, no reliance on color alone to convey match status.
